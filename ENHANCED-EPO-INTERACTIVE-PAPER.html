Abstract

We propose the Entropic Principle of Organization (EPO) as a new foundational framework for physics that addresses the deep crises facing modern cosmology and quantum mechanics. The EPO posits that information is the fundamental substrate of reality, governed by two complementary drives: EPO-I (Integration) toward complexity and order, and EPO-D (Dispersion) toward chaos and entropy. This framework provides novel explanations for dark matter, dark energy, quantum measurement, consciousness emergence, and offers a path toward unifying General Relativity with Quantum Mechanics through informational physics.

Part I: Prolegomena to Informational Physics

Chapter 1: The Ontological Crisis in Modern Physics

1.1. The Limits of the Materialist-Mechanistic Paradigm

Science advances through the iterative refinement of its theories, but it leaps forward through the revolution of its paradigms. The materialist-mechanistic paradigm that has dominated physics for centuries treats reality as a collection of static objects interacting through deterministic forces.

While this framework yielded the twin triumphs of General Relativity and the Standard Model of particle physics, it now faces insurmountable challenges. The problems are no longer mere puzzles to be solved with more precise data—they are symptoms of a deep conceptual failure in our foundational ontology.

1.2. The ΛCDM Crisis: Symptoms of a Deeper Failure

The standard model of cosmology, Lambda-Cold Dark Matter (ΛCDM), stands as a testament to both the precision of modern observation and the depth of our theoretical ignorance. The model requires that approximately 95% of the universe's energy density consists of two entities for which there is no fundamental physical explanation:

  • Cold Dark Matter (CDM): A non-baryonic, non-luminous substance invoked to explain gravitational phenomena that cannot be accounted for by observed matter
  • Dark Energy: The theoretical predictions are catastrophically wrong, exceeding the observed value by up to 120 orders of magnitude
  • Hubble Tension: A persistent, statistically significant discrepancy that has surpassed the 5-sigma "gold standard" for a discovery

1.3. The Unification Impasse

Beneath this cosmological crisis lies a deeper theoretical schism: the incompatibility of General Relativity and Quantum Mechanics. GR describes a smooth, deterministic, geometric reality, while QM describes a discrete, probabilistic, and relational one.

The enduring failure to unify these frameworks, despite decades of effort from theories like String Theory and Loop Quantum Gravity, suggests both may be incomplete approximations of a deeper principle that is neither purely geometric nor purely quantum in the conventional sense.

1.4. The Process-Relational Turn: A New Philosophical Foundation

We propose a paradigm shift rooted in process-relational philosophy that sees reality not as a collection of static things, but as a dynamic flux of process and relation. The EPO framework builds upon this foundation.

The central insight is that information, understood as the pattern and relational structure of process, is the fundamental substance of reality. Matter, energy, space, and time emerge from more basic informational dynamics.

Part II: The Axiomatic Foundations of the EPO

In response to the aforementioned crisis, we propose a new foundation for physics built upon three clear, fundamental axioms. These axioms define the ontology of the Entropic Principle of Organization.

Axiom I: The Primacy of Information

The fundamental, irreducible substrate of reality is information. This is not information in the abstract, but is defined functionally:

Definition: Information is any signal, projection, or potential that has the capacity to cause a change in the state of integration of a system.

Under this axiom, matter, energy, space, and time are not primary entities but are emergent, relational properties of underlying informational processes. A physical system is, at its most basic level, an informational structure.

Axiom II: The Principle of Entropic Duality

The evolution of all information in the universe is governed by a single, dual-aspected fundamental principle. This is the central engine of the EPO framework, positing two complementary and ceaselessly interacting drives:

EPO-D: The Dispersive Drive

The universal tendency towards the dispersal of energy, proliferation of accessible microstates, and homogeneity. This drive manifests as cosmic expansion, thermal equilibrium, and the thermodynamic arrow of time.

EPO-I: The Integrative Drive

The universal tendency towards integration of information, compression of complexity into ordered structures, and formation of stable systems. This drive powers all structurogenesis, from atoms to galaxies to life.

FEPO = -ζi∇Ui + ζd∇Ud
The fundamental EPO Force Law unifying integration and dispersion

Axiom III: The Universe as a Closed, Self-Referential System

The EPO posits that the universe is the total, encompassing system, with no external "meta-environment." Its evolution is entirely self-contained, governed by the internal dialectic of the Entropic Duality.

This self-contained nature ultimately implies that the universe is a self-observing system, where the information being processed and the processor of that information are one and the same, leading to ontological closure and the emergence of cosmic consciousness.

Part III: The Mechanics of Entropic Interaction

Chapter 3: From Principle to Force: The EPO Interaction

3.1. A Truly Fundamental Interaction

A principle, to be physically meaningful, must have a mechanism. The dynamic interplay between the Dispersive and Integrative drives is not passive bookkeeping—it is an active, fundamental interaction governing all system evolution.

We propose this interplay is mediated by a new fundamental force: the Entropic Principle of Organization (EPO) Interaction.

3.2. The Entropic Potential Field (EPF)

Like other fundamental interactions, the EPO Interaction is mediated by a universal field permeating all reality. We term this the Entropic Potential Field (EPF). Unlike other fields, the EPF is intrinsically dualistic.

At every point in spacetime, the EPF possesses two complementary potentials:

Integrative Potential (Ui)

An attractive potential—a "well" in the EPF that drives systems towards states of higher integration, complexity, and causal irreducibility.

Dispersive Potential (Ud)

A repulsive potential—a "hill" in the EPF that drives systems towards states of greater dispersal, homogeneity, and thermal equilibrium.

Part IV: The Explanatory Power of the EPO

Chapter 6: The Emergence of Spacetime and Gravity

The EPO does not take spacetime and gravity as given—it explains their origin as emergent properties of more fundamental EPO dynamics.

6.1. Spacetime as an EPO-I Phase Transition

The EPO provides a causal principle for the emergence of our stable, (3+1)-dimensional spacetime manifold from a pre-geometric, high-entropy phase. The formation of familiar spacetime was a necessitated cosmological phase transition, driven by the EPO-I drive seeking optimal balance between informational complexity and structural stability.

6.2. Gravity as an Information Field

EPO's theory of gravity follows directly from Entropic Potential Field mechanics. In our refined understanding, mass measures a system's capacity to integrate information, and gravity is the interaction between these integrative capacities.

Chapter 7: The Nature of Time and Quantum Reality

7.1. A Unified Arrow of Time

The EPO provides a unified explanation for time's arrow through the fundamental asymmetry in the Entropic Duality. Time's direction is defined by the net flow of information between integration and dispersion phases.

7.2. Quantum Mechanics: Cascading Phase Shifts

Quantum mechanics emerges as cascading information phase shifts. Wave functions represent information potential states, and measurement represents EPO-I integration events that collapse possibilities into actuality.

Chapter 8: Resolving Cosmological Conundrums

8.1. Dark Energy: Local Triumph, Global Consequence

Dark energy emerges naturally from EPO-D dynamics. The accelerating cosmic expansion is not driven by exotic energy but by the dispersive aspect of the fundamental entropic duality operating at cosmological scales.

8.2. Dark Matter: Gravitational Effect of Information

Dark matter is not exotic particles but the gravitational manifestation of information density that has not undergone EPO-I phase transition to form ordinary matter. It provides the informational scaffolding for cosmic structure formation.

Chapter 9: The Decisive Test: The Bullet Cluster

The Bullet Cluster collision provides a crucial test of EPO theory. The spatial separation between visible matter and gravitational lensing effects aligns perfectly with EPO predictions about information density distributions during high-energy cosmic events.
